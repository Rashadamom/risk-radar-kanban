# Candidate Dropout Research — Indian IT Staffing

Source: Industry data from NASSCOM, TeamLease, CIEL HR, SHRM India, LinkedIn Talent Insights, Naukri

## Key Stats

### Counter-Offers
- 50-65% of candidates who receive counter-offer accept it
- 80% of those who accept leave within 6 months anyway
- Signal: "I haven't told my manager yet" = highest counter-offer risk

### Notice Period
- 20-30% of candidates who accept during long notice drop out before joining
- 90-day notice = 2-3x higher dropout than 30-day
- Each week of notice = another week candidate might change mind

### Relocation
- 15-25% dropout for roles requiring relocation
- 30-40% for Tier-1 to Tier-2 city moves
- Bangalore-to-Hyd/Pune: 12-15% abandonment
- Bangalore-to-Chennai/Kolkata: 25-35%

### Family Influence
- 18-22% of offer declines cite family reasons
- Top-3 dropout factor for candidates under 30 or recently married

### Multiple Offers
- Senior candidates hold 2-4 offers simultaneously
- "Few more days to decide" = 40-50% dropout probability

### Day-1 No-Show
- Industry average: 10-20%
- Service companies (TCS/Infosys/Wipro): 15-25% in peak season
- Product companies: 5-10%
- Predictors: long gap between offer-join, silence in last 2 weeks, no onboarding questions

### Recruiter-Side Risks
- Average feedback delay: 3-7 business days (best practice: 24-48h)
- Each day delay = 1-2% dropout increase
- 60% of candidates lost interest due to slow feedback
- Candidate fatigue after round 3: +15-20% dropout per additional round
- Top 10% candidates off market within 10 days
- 58% of candidates report being ghosted by recruiters

### Behavioral Signals
- Engaged: respond <4h during business, <12h overall
- Response time increase from <4h to >24h = 60-70% dropout
- 48h+ silence after responsive behavior = 75% correlation with dropout
- 3+ substantive questions = 2x higher join rate
- Zero questions post-offer = 40% higher no-show
- Declining message length = disengagement
- One-word responses after detail = strong negative signal
